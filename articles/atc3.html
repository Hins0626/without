3 external treatment methods for treating or preventing fever

Last updated: Loading...




Attention! While there is a chance that fever can be addressed with external treatment methods, it does not mean this symptom should be taken lightly. If the fever does not subside within a few hours after using the following methods or persists after a night's sleep, please make sure to continue using medication.




1.Gentle Stroking Method for Reducing Fever in Children. Parents should wash their hands and expose the child's arms. Using their fingers, gently stroke the skin on the child's arms, following the route from the midpoint of the wrist to the midpoint of the elbow. Then, lift the fingers and return to the midpoint of the wrist without repeated strokes. Perform this stroking method approximately 500 times. Both arms can be stroked simultaneously. While it may not provide an immediate cooling effect, it may help shorten the duration of the fever. If there are no fever-reducing medications available, adults can also try this method.





2.Before a child develops a fever, when their body temperature is still normal, if the child's hands and feet feel cold, and their ears are cold, using methods such as friction, warm compress, soaking in hot water, etc., to restore normal temperature and blood circulation in the hands, feet, and ears, can help prevent the onset of fever. If during an outbreak of illness, adults suddenly feel their hands and feet getting cold and start to experience chills, timely warming methods can also be employed to prevent fever.



3.Acupoint stimulation. As shown in the diagram, press and stimulate the acupoints indicated by the red dots, located at the junction of the index and middle fingers, and the junction of the little finger and ring finger. Massage each acupoint approximately 300 times. While it may not provide an immediate cooling effect, it may help shorten the duration of the fever.
